# Salescraft — Product Vision

## Mission

Salescraft exists to give a commercial flooring company an unfair advantage in winning school and government contracts. It does this by:

1. **Finding opportunities before competitors** — monitoring bond measures, capital improvement plans, building age data, and public meeting agendas to identify flooring projects months before they appear on bid boards
2. **Building genuine relationships** — proactively surfacing personal interests, life events, and commonalities so every interaction strengthens trust with facility directors, procurement officers, and decision-makers
3. **Responding faster and better** — AI-assisted proposal writing, automated compliance assembly, and intelligent bid/no-bid decisions
4. **Executing flawlessly** — coordinating from award through installation with real-time field communication

## Core Philosophy: Relationships Win Bids

Government flooring sales is a relationship business. The lowest bid doesn't always win — approved vendor lists, cooperative purchasing, and relationship-driven sole-source work mean that the company with the strongest relationships gets more opportunities and wins more often.

Salescraft treats relationship-building as a first-class capability:
- Before every interaction, the system surfaces what you know about the person — their hobbies, their kids, the boat they just bought, the sports team they follow
- After every interaction, the system captures new personal details learned
- The system proactively suggests reasons to reach out: "Opening day is next week — 6 contacts in your territory are baseball fans"
- Sales reps can reverse-search: "Show me contacts who enjoy fishing" and plan a relationship-building event
- Relationship health decays over time — the system alerts when key relationships need attention

This is not "nice to have" — this IS the product's competitive moat.

## Target Market

### Primary Customer Profile
- **Industry:** Commercial flooring installation
- **Customers:** K-12 school districts, community colleges, city governments, county governments, state facilities
- **Geography:** Regional (operates in specific territories, typically 1-3 states)
- **Company size:** 20-100 employees (sales team of 3-10, estimating team of 2-5, PMs, field crews)
- **Revenue:** $5M-$50M annual
- **Sales cycle:** 1-18 months (from opportunity discovery to contract award)

### Why Schools & Government
- Predictable funding cycles (bonds, annual budgets, state programs)
- Public information (budgets, meeting agendas, bid postings are all public record)
- Repeat business (buildings need new flooring every 15-25 years)
- Relationship-driven (approved vendor lists, cooperative purchasing, relationship-based sole-source)
- Large project sizes ($50K-$2M per project)

## User Personas

### Sales Rep (Alex)
- **Day:** Prospecting, relationship building, attending pre-bid meetings, site visits, trade shows
- **Needs:** Know who to call, why to call them, what to talk about, which bids are worth pursuing
- **Pain today:** Tracks contacts in spreadsheets, misses bid deadlines, forgets personal details, doesn't know about projects until they hit bid boards
- **Success metric:** Win rate, pipeline value, relationship depth (multi-threaded accounts)

### Estimator (Maria)
- **Day:** Reviewing floor plans, calculating materials, pricing labor, building proposals
- **Needs:** Accurate takeoffs, current pricing, fast proposal turnaround, historical data on similar projects
- **Pain today:** Manual PDF measurements, chasing pricing updates from manufacturers, rebuilding proposals from scratch
- **Success metric:** Estimate accuracy (vs. actuals), proposal turnaround time, win rate on bid proposals

### Project Manager (Dave)
- **Day:** Coordinating crews, managing schedules, handling change orders, quality inspections
- **Needs:** Real-time field updates, schedule visibility, document management, change order tracking
- **Pain today:** Phone tag with field crews, paper punch lists, scattered project documents
- **Success metric:** On-time completion, margin preservation, customer satisfaction, repeat business

### Field Installer (Carlos)
- **Day:** On job sites installing flooring, documenting progress, noting issues
- **Needs:** Site access info, scope of work, material specs, ability to log time/progress/issues from phone
- **Pain today:** Paper timesheets, calling PM for every question, no easy way to document problems with photos
- **Success metric:** Daily production (sq ft), quality (punch list items), safety incidents

### Sales Manager / Owner (Patricia)
- **Day:** Pipeline reviews, bid approvals, strategy, resource allocation, customer relationships
- **Needs:** Pipeline visibility, win/loss trends, rep activity, capacity planning, margin analysis
- **Pain today:** No single view of pipeline, relies on reps' verbal updates, can't forecast accurately
- **Success metric:** Revenue growth, win rate, margin, team utilization

## Competitive Positioning

### What Salescraft Is NOT
- **Not Salesforce** — not a generic CRM that must be customized. Salescraft is purpose-built for commercial flooring + government procurement from day one
- **Not a bid service** — bid boards like BidNet find published bids. Salescraft finds opportunities BEFORE they become bids and gives you the relationship advantage to win them
- **Not project management only** — tools like Procore manage construction. Salescraft covers the entire lifecycle from opportunity discovery through project close-out
- **Not a generic AI wrapper** — the AI understands flooring products, government procurement rules, prevailing wage, bonding requirements, and school district buying patterns

### Unique Value
1. **Opportunity discovery** — find projects 3-12 months before competitors by monitoring bonds, CIPs, meeting agendas, and building age
2. **Relationship intelligence** — surface personal connections, interests, and life events that turn cold outreach into warm conversations
3. **Domain expertise baked in** — understands waste factors for LVT vs. carpet tile, knows Davis-Bacon wage determinations, handles bid bond requirements automatically
4. **Full lifecycle** — one system from "this school might need flooring" to "warranty registered, schedule maintenance follow-up in 5 years"

## Success Metrics

### Leading Indicators
- Opportunities discovered before bid posting (# and $ value)
- Relationship health scores across top accounts
- Time-to-proposal (days from bid posting to submission)
- Pre-bid meeting attendance rate
- Contact interactions per week (across all channels)

### Lagging Indicators
- Bid win rate (% and $ value)
- Revenue per sales rep
- Gross margin per project
- Customer lifetime value (repeat business)
- Estimate accuracy (bid price vs. actual costs)

## System Boundaries

### In Scope
- Contact and company management (with relationship intelligence)
- Communication tracking (email, phone, in-person, social)
- Project intelligence (opportunity discovery, bid monitoring)
- Government procurement knowledge (compliance, ethics, purchasing rules)
- Flooring estimating (takeoffs, materials, labor, proposals)
- Bid response management (calendar, assembly, submission tracking)
- Project lifecycle (award through close-out and warranty)
- AI capabilities (scoring, discovery, conversation intelligence, proposal writing)
- Mobile field app (offline-first for installers)
- Dashboards and reporting

### Out of Scope
- Full accounting / ERP (integrates with QuickBooks/Sage but doesn't replace)
- HR / payroll (tracks hours for job costing but doesn't do payroll)
- Inventory management (tracks material orders per project but isn't a warehouse system)
- Marketing automation (not doing email drip campaigns to thousands — this is relationship selling to known contacts)
- Subcontractor management beyond project-level coordination

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| IFB | Invitation for Bid — formal sealed bid process |
| RFP | Request for Proposal — evaluated on qualifications + price |
| RFQ | Request for Quote — informal quote below threshold |
| Prevailing Wage | Government-mandated minimum wage rates by trade and county |
| Davis-Bacon | Federal prevailing wage law for projects >$2,000 |
| Cooperative Purchasing | Programs (Sourcewell, OMNIA, NASPO) that let agencies buy without bidding |
| Sole Source | Direct purchase without competition (requires justification) |
| CIP | Capital Improvement Plan — multi-year facility spending plan |
| Takeoff | Measuring square footage from floor plans for estimating |
| Waste Factor | Extra material needed beyond measured area (cuts, patterns, mistakes) |
| LVT | Luxury Vinyl Tile — popular resilient flooring |
| VCT | Vinyl Composition Tile — traditional commercial tile |
| Wear Layer | Top protective layer of vinyl flooring (measured in mils) |
| Punch List | List of deficiencies to correct before project acceptance |
| Close-out | Final documentation package after project completion |
| NTP | Notice to Proceed — official project start authorization |
| Bid Bond | Guarantee that bidder will accept contract if awarded (typically 10% of bid) |
| Performance Bond | Guarantee of project completion (typically 100% of contract value) |
| Approved Vendor List | Pre-qualified list of vendors who can receive work without full bid process |
| Multi-threaded | Having relationships with multiple people in an organization (not dependent on one contact) |
